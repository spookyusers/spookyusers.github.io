Selected Projects

Michelson interferometer measurements

Built, aligned, and operated a Michelson interferometer to perform precision optical measurements. Used controlled mirror translation and fringe frequency analysis to measure the wavelength of a He-Ne laser, and quantified changes in refractive index of air under varying pressure using a gas cell. Additional measurements included determining the refractive index of a quartz slab via slab rotation. The work involved photodiode and oscilloscope data acquisition, fringe counting, uncertainty propagation, and analysis of systematic experimental limitations.

Zeeman Effect in Mercury and Neon Spectra

Measured magnetic field induced splitting of atomic emission lines in mercury and neon using a high-resolution Czerny-Turner grating spectrometer. Resolved both normal and anomalous Zeeman patterns and extracted effective Lande g-factors from Gaussian fits to split spectral lines. Achieved agreement with theory at the 1-5$ level, including geff ~2.0 for the normal Zeeman triplet and reduced g-factors for anomalous transitions. Verified linear dependence of wavelength splitting on magnetic field strength and used neon data to experimentally determine the electron charge-to-mass ratio.

Faraday Rotation and Verdet Constant Measurement

Observed magneto-optical Faraday rotation in multiple glass samples under longitudinal magnetic fields. Established Malus's Law baselines for polarized light transmission and measured rotation angles as a function of applied field strength. Extracted Verdet constants for three materials at red, yellow, and blue wavelengths, confirming linear Faraday rotation (Ï• = V B d) and the expected increase in magnitude at shorter wavelengths. Data acquisition and analysis were performed using photodiode measurements and Python nonlinear fitting and regression.

433 MHz Band Sensor Decoding with RTL-SDR

Used software-defined radio (RTL-SDR dongle) and rtl_433 to passively receive and decode 433.92 MHz transmissions from consumer wireless devices in the local area. Successfully captured and identified signals from multiple indoor/outdoor temperature/humidity sensors, soil moisture/temperature probes, and a DSC wireless security door/window contact. Built rtl_433 from source on Linux, tuned a dipole antenna, documented setup, raw captures, and analysis.

Automated Two-Slit Interference Scanning

Designed and built an automated scanning system for Young's double-slit experiment using a motorized spinning mirror, photodiode, and oscilloscope. Spinning mirror reflects laser across a photodiode sensor to display the intensity of the overall interference pattern on the oscilloscope.
