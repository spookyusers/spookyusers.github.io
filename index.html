Experimental Physics

Michelson interferometer measurements

Built, aligned and operated a Michelson interferometer for precision optical measurements. Measured the wavelength of a He-Ne laser and the refractive index of air under varying pressure via fringe analysis, and determined the refractive index of a quartz slab using slab rotation, with photodiode and oscilloscope based data acquisition and uncertainty analysis.

Lab report (PDF)

Zeeman Effect in Mercury and Neon Spectra

Measured magnetic field induced splitting of atomic emission lines in mercury and neon using a high resolution Czerny-Turner grating spectrometer. Resolved both normal and anomolous Zeeman patterns, extracted effective Lande g-factors from Gaussian fits to split spectral lines, verified linear splitting with magnetic field, and used neon data to experimentally determine the electron charge to mass ratio, achieving agreement with theory at the 1-5% level.

Lab report (PDF)

Faraday Rotation and Verdet Constant Measurement

Measured magneto-optical Faraday rotation in multiple glass samples and extracted wavelength dependent Verdet constants using photodiode measurements and Python based fitting, confirming linear Faraday rotation.

Lab report (PDF)

Independent / Technical Projects

433 MHz Band Sensor Decoding with RTL-SDR

Used a software-defined radio (RTL-SDR) and rtl_433 to passively receive and decode 433.92 MHz transmissions from consumer wireless devices. Captured and identified signals from environmental sensors and a wireless security contact, building rtl_433 from source on Linux, tuning a dipole antenna, and documenting raw captures and analysis.

GitHub repository

Automated Two-Slit Interference Scanning

Designed and built an automated scanning system for Young's double-slit experiment using a motorized spinning mirror, photodiode, and oscilloscope. The rotating mirror swept the inteference pattern across a fixed detector, enabling a real-time visualization of fringe intensity on the oscilloscope.
